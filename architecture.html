Taoist architecture is usually composed of temples, dining halls, dormitories and gardens. Its overall layout basically adopts the traditional Chinese courtyard style, that is, wooden frame as the main structure, single building composed of "room" as a unit, and then single building composed of courtyard, and then courtyard as a unit to form various forms of architectural complex. Temple is the main place of religious activities, often located on the main axis of the architectural complex, as the main body of the entire architectural complex.

The core concepts of Taoism are: human law, earth law, heaven law and nature law. Taoism advocates nature and believes that the natural landscape is the main body of all things, and the existence of all things as objects depends on "mountains, forests, valleys and hills". Taoism has the belief of immortals and the belief of mountain worship. That the mountain is a ladder, up and down the heaven; There is god on the mountain, and there is zhi Cao on the mountain. In order to cultivate the immortal, in order to refine the elixir of immortality, mountain, Taoist palace has become the preferred external environment. Therefore, Taoist architecture pays more attention to creating the atmosphere of immortal road, immortal spirit and fairyland. Pay attention to the heart, hope chang God yu zhi. The layout of the building pays particular attention to feng shui. The architectural style contains the situation of "taiji evolution", "harmony between the two instruments" and "the universe and the universe are determined". At the same time, it also reflects the artistic conception of Taoist magic, which is convenient to communicate with the gods and achieve the fairy way self.
